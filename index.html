Liam Payne

Former member of One Direction

Skynews photo

Here's a time-line of Liam Payne's career

  • 2008-2015 - Liam Payne first auditioned for The X Factor in 2008 at 14 but was cut. He returned in 2010, impressing judges and later joining Harry Styles, Niall Horan, Louis Tomlinson, and Zayn Malik to form One Direction. The group gained immense popularity, finishing third on The X Factor and signing with Simon Cowell's label. Their debut single "What Makes You Beautiful" was a global hit, followed by several successful albums and tours. Payne became one of the band's primary songwriters, and after Zayn Malik left in 2015, Payne took over many of his vocal parts. One Direction went on hiatus in 2016 after five albums, selling millions of records and touring worldwide.

  • 2016-2021 - Liam Payne began his solo career as a producer under the monikers "Big Payno" and "Payno," remixing tracks like Cheryl's "I Don't Care." In 2014, he created Hampton Music Limited for his solo projects and co-directed One Mode Productions, which signed 5 Seconds of Summer. Payne collaborated with various artists, including Juicy J, DJ Mustard, Pharrell Williams, and Will Singe. In 2017, he released his debut solo single "Strip That Down," followed by "Bedroom Floor" and "For You" with Rita Ora. His debut album LP1 came out in December 2019, followed by collaborations like "Midnight" with Alesso in 2020 and "Sunshine" for the film Ron's Gone Wrong in 2021.

  • 2023-2024 - In May 2023, Liam Payne announced work on his second album, with more creative control than his debut. He scheduled a solo tour for September 2023 but postponed it due to a kidney infection. Payne returned with the single "Teardrops" on 1 March 2024, co-written with NSYNC's JC Chasez, reaching number 39 on the UK Singles Downloads Chart.
